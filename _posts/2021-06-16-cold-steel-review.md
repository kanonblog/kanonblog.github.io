<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-A"><h2>Highlight of the Game</h2></div></p>

1. 3D Graphic Evolution

    Cold steel 1-2 show normal quality in 3D, while Cold steel 3-4 improve their grapgic largely. The movement goes smoother, and the texture are shown very decent. The use of color looks great too.

2. Good Music Quailty

    The sound tracks of Cold Steel series are quite good. You may enjoy the music along the journey in the game. 


<br><br>
<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-B"><h2>Game Review to Trails of Cold Steel 1-4</h2></div></p>

1. Cold Steel 1-2

    - I treat them a set of game, in terms of the flow of story.

    - (**Spoiler** in this point) A Very Rough Story Flow: The story focuses on the protagonist Rean's schooling period. Rean meets his allies, and know each other better during the journey around Erebonian Empire.

    - These 2 games are more an introduction about the background information of the story, including Rean's interpersonal relationship.

    - Players are able to control Rean to build up bond with characters. Some events are designed to interact with the characters. The feeling of being a student can be represented in those events.

    - If you are not a big fan of Falcom, and you are not very interested on the school life of Rean, you may skip this game. The game flow should be mentioned along with the new launched games (I am not sure), or you may try to have a look of brief story online. Personally, I think there is no much story progress made.

    - It is a long series. Your taste on characters may change largely along with the progress of the story. 

<br>

2. Cold Steel 3-4

    - I treat these games a set.

    - (**Spoiler** in this point) Rean gets to be an instructor instead of a student. He proceeds the story with his allies from his school life, and his students.

    - Personally I prefer Cold Steel 3 greater than 4, but the ending somehow seem to be good in Cold Steel 4.

    - You may enjoy this game better by focusing on the character interaction. 

    - If you are interested to Hajimari no kiseki instead of Cold Steel, you are okay to skip. The whole journey of Cold Steel should be described in Hajimari no kiseki (I am not sure), or you may take a look online.

<br><br>
<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-C"><h2>My Thoughts to Bond Events</h2></div></p>

I am **not sure whether this is a kind of spoiler**. If you want to avoid potential spoiler, please skip this part.

I don't feel every good about bond events... I feel like each character is not described very well in the main story, and I doubt whether the bond events limit the character description in the main story. I feel every good for Hajimari no kiseki to have no bond events.

<br><br>
<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-D"><h2>Last words</h2></div></p>

I do have fun in Trails of Cold Steel series. It has been a very long series, and it accompanies myself for several years. I hope the next kiseki can make better description in main story, while the character interaction can remain good.
