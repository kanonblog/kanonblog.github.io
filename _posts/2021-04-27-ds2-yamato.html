Overall Review

Default members in this route: Yamato, Makoto, Keita, Fumi

They would join the team regardless of the bonding level. The main story is proceeded by these members and you will see their conversations.

Positive Feedback:

Finally I can see Yamato to join! His ability seems useful something when it produces more damages through both physical and magical attacks.

The default members are more described in the route, because they get more opportunities to talk.

This route seems to be the easiest route in terms of battle?


Negative Feedback:

Yamato seems to have higher level and better demons before entering this route....

Although there are different routes available in the game, the story is quite similar according to the information of game wiki. The enemies and stages look quite alike after the routes selection. It would be quite repeatitive to walk through each route...


For the main story:

I think all default members except Makato are really capable or aiming high in their expertise. Yamato has the special power to control Dragon Stream. Fumi is good at computing. Keita aims to get stronger in boxing. Their common attribute is having less desire to build bond or resonating the feelings with others.

I think Makoto is different than other members, because she has strong desire to help weaks throughout the story, and she always try to care or resonate the feelings of others. In my opnion, if people have these kinds of attributes, they will tend to choose other routes, like Akie or Otome. If I am going to proceed the remaining routes, I may draft another article to describe the difference of their choices.

This is quite ironic to see the ending in this route:

Yamato asserted his dislike towards the modern society. For example, he did not want to see people having more power in authorities bullying the ones with strong abilities. He did not wanna see strong people sacrifacing themselves in exchange to seek harmonious among others.

However, who can have the right to judge the true strength of others?

At the end, the world remains to the status of full of demons, instead of returning to the normal life.

The world seems to run under the rule of meritocracy. The ones with stronger power gain the right to speak loud, especially for the ones with strong survival capability.

Even a child can be a lead of the whole team. I don't think it is wrong in this part.

However, isn't Yamato doing the same thing he hate to see?

Yamato is the lead of organization JP's. The power and resources are centralized. He is now having strongest power under this situation. He takes this advantage and get well prepared than others. This is already inequlity in the competitive line.

The strength is determined by the ability to survive in this route, but won't there have other types of strength?

I think it is interesting when people have their own strength in a dedicated area, while they may have flaws in other fields. It is not fair to judge the personal capability based on survival skills only, and ignore personal attributes in other fields. People with underrated skills may need to please the ones with stronger survival skills (or show the value to them). I don't think there are huge changes in this social model compared to the previous society model described by Yamato.

Yamato seems becoming the one who he initially disagreed.



Main characters in this route

Yamato

I said most of the review towards him in the story section.

I do think he is a good superior in an organization. He can prioritize the importance of the things, instead of just judging a situation with his own feeling. He fire one of the staff after reaching the end, instead of doing it during the fight against Septentrione. This may prevent the loss of motivation among the staff.

Also, he can observe the value of others and put attention to people with good performance, such as the protagoist. People with strength like Fumi are willing to pursue him.

I kinda think I may work with excellent performance under the ruling of Yamato.

However, he somehow shows his cruelty in the main story, such as fighting against the weak without hestitation.

The less resonation to others lead to his choice of meritocracy. I think this part is described with a larger extent in the animation.


Makoto

She is the only one with higher extent of kindness in this route.

I think the main reason for she to join is to repay the kindness given by JP's for granting she a new life, as she seeks her lifelong goal in the organization.

It is just she is not the kind of people truely advocate meritocracy.

She even feel bad when she witness the situation of one soilder looking down another fired soilder during the end.

The reason of her attitude is about the feeling of helping the weak and caring others.

She cares others throughout the whole story regardless of their status, such as treating the protagonist and strangers really good compared to other JP's staff.

There is the reason why Ronaldo invites her to cooperate.

I think it is quite difficult to take own decisions in an organization like this, because there should be a lot of rules and burdens. I appreciate her so much when I grow up to know this. Therefore, I really respect her determinations throughout the game.


Fumi

She is a cool, beatiful, clever, and outstanding lady.

She is like a person with all answers and barely need helps from others.

I get a huge impact while I heard her assertion; She said she was so poor, and she was able to contiune studying when she truely wanted to find a solution.

I think the reason of her advocation to meritocracy is because she is simply too capable.

She gets rated in the JP's, and she may think own capability would be a good judgement towards others as well.

I guess she may encountered a lot of trials behind screen, so I do not have much to add regarding to her advocation.

Anyway, she is a truely skillful person who is good at demon fighting, computing, and finding answers with few clues.


Keita

I was surprised to his choice towards meritocracy, but I acknowledged while I knew his character.

He is a guy only pursuing strength.

He is only 16 years old and may not have a lot of experience in different fields, so he may not realize the value of hanging around with others.

His own value or self-esteem may be built upon the strength.

Although he does not act very nice to commrades, he is still kind in some degree, referring to the conversation with Jungo in his bonding event.

I don't think he is a bad guy. His reaction may come due to his age.



Last Words

I completed this route first because Yamato is really a good superior. He is indeed a capable person, but he has his own shortcomings as well.

I hope I will finish some routes in some days and report the game reflection in this blog~~
