<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-A"><h2>Highlights</h2></div></p>

1. Fan-Disc, but a good one.

    The storylines are related to Trails From Zero (Zero-no-kiseki), Trails to Azure (Ao-no-kiseki) and Trails of Cold Steel (Sen-no-Kiseki) series. The protagonists of these games have two storylines in this game. Players can be relatable when they have the knowledge of previous games. It is enjoyable to see the interaction among old characters.

2. Good music as usual

   This game remains high quality in music as usual. Here is my favourite music in this game.

3. Parallel Storylines

    This is the new system in trails series. Lloyd, Rean and C are the main protagonists in the stories. They have their story events in the timeline, and they generally act separately for their tasks. It is interesting to trace their actions in the certain time point. 

4. Additional side stories

    There are side story focused on few character(s). It solves the problem of the main story always having a lot of people, and sometimes the characters may not be well described. I didn't read all side stories, because it needed extra effort to unlock, but some stories looked interesting.

5. Mini-game

    I am not a fan on built-in mini-game, but Magical Alisa is somehow interesting. The game itself is not attractive to me, but the story development is kind of like the magical anime broadcast in morning, and it looks interesting. I only finished some of them, so I cannot say it well. You may have a try if you are interested.

6. Dungeon alike to Trails in the Sky 3rd, but an improved one.

    Infinity Corridor is a dungeon opened along with the game progress. It is a bit similar to the one in Trails in the Sky 3rd. The similar functions are character side-stories and trials, but some attractive points are added.

    - All playable members gather regardless of the story progress, while the main story reaches some time point. Players can talk to their favorite characters if the characters are playable. It is the same for the main protagonists, players can control other playable characters to talk to the main protagonists. The slight changes in the conversations may happen dependent on the controlling character, and it may be attractive for fans to collect dialogue. 

    - Playable characters can be summoned to the Infinity Corridor, including the opponents in the main story. I was surprised to see McBurn turning to be a playable character, and his abilities are so good. His equipments can be seen through the menu as well. 

    - Outfits can be obtained, including the old ones. When I try to put the Cold Steel II outfit to Rean, it looks kind of... No. Rean grows so much in his appearance, and the old outfit looks no longer suitable.

    - Infinity Corridor is relevant to the main story. 

7. The new chatacters are well described.

    The main focus seems to be Lloyd and Rean at first glance, but the new characters are well described. The stoylines are isolated for a period, so the characters can interact among few members. It is quite astonishing that they are all new characters, and a storyline is not so long, but their characteristics are impressive.

<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-B"><h2>(Spoiler) Personal Thoughts</h2></div></p>

This part contains **spoiler**, and the main story may get revealed largely. Please be ready before you read.

I am not certain about the English name of the new characters, so please allow me to quote the Japanese name along with the translated name.

1. The parallel storyline system is so cool

    The BGM is so cool, and the moment of switching the storyline is so cool. Although the story gets locked in a certain time point, and there is no much options available, the feeling of jumping into a new part of the story is quite good. Sometimes the story is developed excited, while the storyline is mandatory to get changed. 

2. The plot of "C" is interesting

    It was hard to guess the identity of "C" before release. It was hard before I heard his true voice as well, because there was too many characters in Trails of Cold Steel series. The reason of being "C" is explained in the game, and I think it looks reasonable.

3. C is a rare type of protagonist in trails series

    His age is not as young as Rean and Lloyd, and his charateristics are unique among protagonists. Who can predict a relatively minor opponent turnning into a protagonist suddenly?

4. The new characters are impressive in C's Route

    - Each team tends to have less members compared to previous series, so more characters are described well.

    - Swin (スウィン) and Nadia (ナーディア) works in a group initially, and they rely each other to be perfect in terms of abilities. They always stay together, and it may get to be a problem when they get separated. That sounds reasonable, and the past of the characters are well mentioned along with the story. 

    - Rufus and Lapis(ラピス) meet in this game, but I prefer to treat Rufus a new character in this game. Although Rufus appeared in the first game of Trails of Cold Steel, his inner mind was not mentioned according to my memory. This is the first time his past and struggles appear. He always looked powerful to achieve things for his own sake, while he had some thoughts to his young brother Jusis. I think this character settings sound a lot more human than the setup in Cold Steel. 

    - Both pairs seem to solve the struggles of each other very well in this route, which makes the character interaction good.

5. Good to have no bond events this time

    Bond events kind of restrict the story development and the character interaction in Cold Steel. For example, the description of Alisa is limited as well as the Reinford Group. Crow appears a lot in the story, but his past as "C" is not very important in the story progress. A lot of background settings in the story seem to be replacable. However, in this game, the characters follow their own solid belief(s) to proceed the story, which I credit this due to the disappearance of bond events. Also, bond events are like a restriction to character interaction. For the original Class VII, the friendship is shown between Laura and Fie, Millium and Jusis, Machias and Jusis, Elliot and Gaius. The remaining members seem to be bound into the concept of being a member of class VII, and help each other, instead of having a solid friendship. Maybe it is just something happening behind the screen, but I prefer to see them in the main story. Another example is the new Class VII. I guess the initial plot is to develop solid friendship between Juna and Kurt , Musse and Ash. However it seems to disapear in the following game Cold Steel 4, and the reason may be the bond events to Rean. The scenes of male characters Kurt and Ash seem to be reduced in Cold Steel 4, excluding the repeatitively lines. The disappearance of bond events should bring better result to the story in my opinion, or the bond events should be improved to prevent the loss of main story description.

6. Good to see Lloyd again

    It has been long time ago from the release of Trails from Zero and Trails to Azure. It is good to see Lloyd again as a protagonist instead of a side character. The independence movement is skipped in Azure, but this game enables players to read more details. The story reminds me that Lloyd is charismatic with his positivity.

7. Comparison is cruel

    Lloyd and Rean should be in the same age, but the team of Lloyd behaves more mature and calm. Lloyd's team is more proactive for their goal, and Rean just behave like to be pushed into a situation as usual. The comparison is cruel.

8. Unity of three protagonists are good

    It feels so excited to read the conversations between 3 protagonists. 

9. Rean's mental development

    - Cold Steel 4 made the game a good ending, and I forgot a lot of plots in the games then. This game reminds me the weird portions of the entire Cold Steel series. The storyline of Rean reminds that Rean tends to make decisions to sacrifice himself and his teammates mentally, and this tendency remains even after the entire Cold Steel series. This implies that Rean's teammates help him to avoid the tragedy, but Rean does not have much changes in mentality at the end of Cold Steel series. I am not trying to blame him, because time is required for improvement, but it looks a bit weird for a long series ending like this.
