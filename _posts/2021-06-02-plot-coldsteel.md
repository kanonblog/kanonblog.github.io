<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-A"><h2>Before the Main Article</h2></div></p>
I had a fun time with cold steel series. The character interaction is so far good, except the bond events. However, I find the main story strange after reaching the end of the cold steel series, including gameplay experience in Hajimari-no-kiseki. I do not watch through all bond events of each character, so the article mentions the observation to main story only.
<br><br>

<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-B"><h2>Strange Points in Main Story</h2></div></p>

- **Olivert in "Trails in the sky 3rd" vs "Trails of Cold Steel"**

    You may review the conversation between Olivert and Osborne.

    <iframe class="center" width="100%" height="400" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Jxdq4mI46kY" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>

    Osborne is apparently an opponent of Olivert. However, Olivert does not have much significant actions to take down him during cold steel series. He kinda leave the confrontation to Rean, and turns into a support role.

- **Build a new class to enforce better culture to empire?**

    I know his intention to improve the current social culture of the empire, but the education should be a long term enforcement, instead of having immediate result. Students do need time to climb into higher positions in the society, for them to change the social ideologies or policies. 
    
    The older generations are the ones to rule the society, and the younger generations may need to endure some injustice. If the social culture can be change as easy as just building a new class, I don't think there need several games to explain the things.

    There is an exception like Crossbell. It faces serious crisis, so everyone needs to change for suvivor. However, main characters in cold steel face crisis while there is a lot of helps, so it seems not applicable.

    I don't mean the age matter in the policy enforcement. The older generations have their wisdom in their lives and they do contribute to make a society operating well. The younger generations may have their insights to make society, or every individual living better. In short, the personality and moral standard matter, rather than age. Please do not bind age groups into stereotypes~~


- **Reinford is left barely mentioned.**

    Reinford is a manufaturer of military weapons. For such a large company, with a lot of earning from military, the incident should not be tackled by only changing the direction of the company. How the monetary flow goes? What responsbilities should they bear? The main story does not mention much, and the detailed descriptions seem to be skipped intentionally. All the Reinford related members mention their company with few words, and seem nothing happened. 

- **Divine Knight in Nord Highlands**

    Stone statues can be observed in Nord Highlands, with unused Divine Knight(s). 

- **Find a Divine Knight in the school by accident**

    How come a old schoolhouse has a unused Divine Knight, and the main protagonist Rean accidentally become its pliot? This kind of story seems like everything going by accident, such like the character development of Rean... 

- **Make acquaintance to some characters in a weird way.**

    For example, Vita suddenly make acquaintance to Rean. Although it may be interpreted that Rean is an important person to Osborne, it still look quite strange without proper follow-up in later story.

- **Conflicts between nobels and commoners**

    This should be an important topic in Erebonian Empire. The different social classes influence their rights. This should be one of the reason for Osborne gaining advocation from commoners. Cold steel 1 mentions the conflicts between class VII and nobel members, but the descriptions are quite few. It looks like the arguments between immature students, instead of having great impact to the general lives in Erebonian Empire.

<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-C"><h2>Characters</h2></div></p>

- **Some main characters generate less impacts than their relatives**

    Elliot - He does not like military stuff and turn to be a musician. His intention is good, but I don't think he make great impact to the main story... Instead, (His father) Olaf has more influence.

    Laura - (Her father) Arseid seems more proactive in the story...

    Machias - (His father) Carl Regnitz has more political power and seem improving the empire in some ways.

    Fie - The members of Zephyr take their actions in the main story, but it does not make sigificant impacts too.

- **Many characters are redundant, or generate nearly no impacts.**

    Alisa - Alisa leaves not much impacts to the main story, because the descriptions of Reinford are few. It was better in cold steel 1.

    Emma - Her role is similar to Vita, but Vita brings more impact to the main story.

    Gaius - He is a very nice guy, and become a Gralsritter later on. However, there is already quite an amount of Gralsritter in the story.

    Sharon - Although she has the title as an Enforcer of Ouroboros, she almost get no mentioning. As stated, almost the Reinford related matters have been described with few words!

- **What is the purpose of loyal families here?**

    They are not very relevant to the story, but get mentioned. I don't think Alfin and Cedric are neccessary in the story, as they are not described in a way very impressive. They are like, they can be substituted by others with the same title. Olivert is fine, but I kinda treat him as a character from "Trails in the sky". He has the same problem of not acting very impressive in cold steel.

- **Imperial Liberation Front**

    It does not do much impacts to the story, even with its appearance in Ao-no-kiseki.
