<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-A"><h2>what you can obtain during the university life?</h2></div></p>

1. You get free time.

    Yes, although you may study really hard, you still get some free time. You may meet friends during the lunch break, or the gap of the lessons. It feels really free and happy to face people with own personality during the school life. 

2. Meet new people

    It depends on the status of your university. If the university is hard to get entered, you tend to meet people with higher standard. I am not going to judge people with their background, and I just mean people may tend to have better personality, such as positive attitude, being more persistent, working hard, willing to share knowldge, handling working schedule good, etc...

    These people may give you good inspiration to tackle with the current studies, or benefit your working style afther the school life. 
    Also, they may give you good ideas to choose a better path in your life.

3. Discover your own interest

    There may be mandatory courses outside the major subjects. You may discover your new interest during a load of studies. It can be tough to start a total new thing in life, but it may get worthy when you look back. 

<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-B"><h2>What are you going to be trained?</h2></div></p>

1. Depends on your "use of time"

    People only have 24 hours per day. Your time management skill controls your work and achievements. If you study harder, you may have less time to do rest of the things. It would be worthy if you can assign your time for something worthy in your perspective.

    For exmaple, If you concentrate on reading textbooks, you can learn the organization or expression of a book. If the books are written for a technical aspect, you may learn how to interpret the books in such a field. After the book reading, you may learn how to handle the reading progress better, by the previous experience.

    If you work hard in a thing positive, it won't be a waste. Good experience can bring positive thinking, and lead you to work positive in other things as well.

2. Depends on your major

    I believe that your major can bring you a certain degree of skills, with the training in the university courses. 

    Please allow me to bring up my study experience in my major. I studied IT in my university course. Generally I needed to duel with the technical issues by myself. I learnt how to solve problems by my own. 

    Also, the deadline was tight, and the workloads seemed hard for me. I needed to work hard in my initiative in order to graduate successfully.

    My language skill is trained by writing articles in English, as I am not a native English speaker.

    I received a career advise from my professor. I was not very eager in coding, so I got adviced to do some intellectual property things. I thought it was not true, but I realized its meaning after I worked in IT field... I am interested in creating content, so I am trying to create some contents right now!

<p><div class="left-margin" id="set-C"><h2>Academic result does not mean much things in a general workplace</h2></div></p>

It is indeed important for you to graduate, as you are responsible to your time and money given. However, the below may be the common thinking trap for university degree, in a general workplace. The opinions are written in the perception of trying hard at study and get slower pace at work.

1. Decent job =/= Good 

    It is quite an excitement when you are able to meet the standard entering a large corporate, and have a decent career path provided there. However, doing good in an university do not neccessarily mean your well being in workplace. Rather than work, your attitude to a work, and relationship management may be another crisis to face. There can be a load of problems to get solved everyday at work, which may depend on your job nature.

2. Decent career perspective =/= enjoyment

    Good career perspective may get interpreted to sacrificing your own time in exchange for advancement. If you want to be a skillful person in a dedicated field, you may need to study hard on examinations, and keep track of new things. You may need to give up your own interest/ time with families/ time with friends/ time to leisure. When you get into a higher position in corporate, you may get more responsibilities, like working over-time or replying an e-mail overnight.
